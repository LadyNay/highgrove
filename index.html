Highgrove Body Corporate Conduct Rules

Date: 19 February 2008

Note: Right of access to Highgrove shall at all times be reserved and shall be at the sole discretion of the Trustees.

1. Access Control

1.1

No persons are to buzz other tenants or owners units' at the gate on the intercom if they have forgotten their remote controls in their units.

1.2

The guards will be allowed to open the gates for entrance to the complex to vehicles displaying the Highgrove disk. Any vehicle not displaying a Highgrove disk will be requested to fill in the visitor's book and entrance shall be sanctioned via the gate intercom to the unit concerned. If no answer is received from a unit via the intercom or no written instruction has been left at the entrance advising of a visitor's visit, access will be denied. The Trustees shall be empowered to alter or modify this rule from time to time as circumstances dictate.

1.3

Access shall be denied to any persons on foot unless the owner or occupier of the section personally signs in such visitor at the security point. Domestic staff members will be required to carry a card signed by the owner of the unit and giving the name of the occupier, ID number, unit number and name of the occupant. New residents can obtain these cards from the supervisor / trustees / managing agent.

1.4

To minimise the security risk at Highgrove it is the duty of owners or residents to inform the supervisor of their contract with workmen, plumbers, electricians, painters, curtaining and / or carpet contractors who move around the property in the performance of their work. These contractors will require a letter from the resident authorising them to work on the relevant unit, and will not be allowed onto the property without authorisation.

1.5

Persons entering or exiting the complex shall be subject to a search by security personnel. Any goods leaving or entering the complex have to be accompanied by the occupier of the unit authorising in writing the removal of goods.

2. Balconies / Patios

2.1

Balconies / patios shall be washed down by bucket only subject to the proviso that the amount of water used shall not cause a torrent of water onto sections or common property below thereby causing damage to sections or occupants of units below.

2.2

The outlet pipes on balconies and patios shall be kept obstruction free by the occupier of the section.

2.3

Residents are requested to use the designated area at the swimming pool for "braais".

3. Behaviour of Children, Employees and Visitors

3.1

Residents shall supervise their children and the children of visitors to ensure that no damage is caused to the common property or nuisance caused to other residents.

3.2

Owners shall ensure that occupants, visitors, guests, employees and children do not interfere with fire hose reels, plants, light fittings, garden furniture, water features, light fittings or any other equipment in Highgrove.

3.3

Owners or occupants of units will be held responsible if they or their visitors, guests, children or employees cause damage or loss of any kind whatsoever in any part of Highgrove.

3.4

Children shall not be allowed to ride tricycles / bicycles and skateboards, roller blades, and the like, or play in the passages of the common property stairway, parking areas or anywhere near the gate entrance of Highgrove. It is requested that children play at the back of the complex and be supervised at all times by an adult / parent. (This rule has been made for the safety of your children) Please ensure that the children do not create a disturbance or noise so as to disturb residents.

3.5

Should the Body Corporate face any legal action or incur any legal expense or penalty as a result of excessive occupancy (particularly in the staff quarters) or misbehaviour by any owner or his employee, lessee, or visitor or guest or child of any of the aforesaid, the owner and / or the persons concerned will be held liable for any cost, loss or damage incurred by the Body Corporate.

4. Building: Appearance from Outside

4.1

The owner or occupier of a unit shall not place or do anything on any part of the common property, including balconies, patios, stoep and gardens, which in the discretion of the Trustees is aesthetically displeasing or undesirable when viewed from the outside of the unit or above.

4.2

No burglar bars or security gates shall be installed without the written permission of the Trustees. The security features have to abide by the Highgrove specification per Annexure A.

5. Building: Damage, Alterations or Additions to the Common Property

5.1

An owner or occupier of a unit shall not mark, paint, drive nails or screws or the like into, or otherwise damage, or alter, any part of the common property without first obtaining the written consent of the Trustees.

5.1 (i)

Notwithstanding the sub above, an owner or person authorised by him, shall install: (a) Any locking device, safety gate, burglar bars or other safety device for the protection of his unit, or (b) Any screen or other device to prevent the entry of animals or insects; Provided that the Trustees have first approved in writing the nature and design of the device.

6. Building: Interior and Exterior Alterations Including Aerials / Satellite Dishes

6.1

Before any interior structural alterations are undertaken to a section, the period within which the work shall be completed shall be agreed with the Trustees and shall be strictly adhered to. A deposit shall be called for from the owner of the unit and will be forfeited if the work is not completed by the deadline or if the common property is damaged.

6.2

Alterations shall be carried out at reasonable hours only (Monday to Friday from 07h30 to 17h00 with no noisy work to be carried out on Saturdays, Sundays or Public Holidays) and shall not cause any undue disturbance to owners or occupiers of neighbouring sections.

6.3

Subject to the above Rules, alterations, additions or decorations to the exterior of the units, including radio / television aerials and satellite dishes, or to exclusive use areas, or to any other portion of the common property, shall not be made without the prior written consent of the Trustees and then only upon the terms and conditions contained in such consent. Should there, in the opinion of the Trustees, be a possibility that an alteration or addition shall affect the strength of the structure or the structural integrity of the building or any servitude or implied servitude as defined in Section 28 of the Sectional Titles Act, 1986, the Trustees shall require a certificate signed by a practising civil engineer certifying that the proposed alterations or additions shall not adversely affect the structure or any servitude or implied servitude before granting their consent to such alteration or addition.

6.4

No structural alteration to interior of units which shall affect the strength of the structure or any servitude or implied servitude as defined in Section 28 of the Sectional Titles Act, 1986, including any alterations to plumbing and electrical installations shall be carried out without prior written consent of the Trustees or the approval of the municipal authorities. The Trustees shall require a certificate signed by a practising civil engineer certifying that the proposed alterations or additions will not adversely affect the structure or any servitude or implied servitude before granting their consent to such alterations or additions.

6.5

An owner or occupier of a unit shall not be entitled to interfere with electrical installations and plug points in the section, unless a licensed technician undertakes such work.

6.6

Interior repairs and maintenance, including geysers and hot water systems, are the responsibility of the owner or occupier of that unit and neither the supervisor, nor the Managing Agent, nor any employee of the Body Corporate are to be requested to attend to such matters.

6.7

In the event of a contractor damaging any part of the common property while doing his work, the damage will be inspected by the supervisor / Trustees, Managing Agent and recorded in writing and signed by the owner or the contractor or the domestic staff member as well as the supervisor. Should it become apparent after the completion of the work by the contractor or others that damage has been caused, the owner concerned shall be responsible to the Body Corporate for the cost of any repairs required, which cost shall be debited to the owner's levy account and collected in the normal manner.

6.8

A resident shall keep and maintain the sewerage pipes, water pipes and drains in his unit free from obstruction and blockages and generally in a good state of repair.

7. Building: Owner's Failure to Maintain

7.1

If an owner: (i) Fails to repair or maintain his unit in a state of good repair as required by the Sectional Titles Act 95 of 1986; or (ii) Fails to maintain adequately any area of the common property allocated for his exclusive use and enjoyment; And any such failure persists for a period of thirty (30) days after the giving of written notice to repair or maintain given by the Trustees or the Managing Agent on their behalf, the Body Corporate shall be entitled to remedy the owner's failure and to recover the reasonable cost of doing so from such owner.

8. Business, Auctions and Jumble Sales

8.1

No business or trade of any nature shall be conducted in a unit or on the common property.

8.2

No auctions or jumble sales shall be held on the common property or in a unit without the written approval of the Trustees.

9. Collection of Levies

9.1

The purpose of a levy is to pay for rates and taxes, electricity, water, effluent, maintenance, employees' salaries, gardens, insurance and generally to maintain the building in a prime state of maintenance; repairing and replacing where desirable or necessary. It is the duty of the Trustees to determine the levy in terms of Management Rule 31.

9.2

It is the duty of all owners to pay the levy promptly in advance when due on the first day of each calendar month.

9.3

Owners are, in addition, liable for payment for services which are individually metered, such as electricity supplied to their units or exclusive use staff quarters.

9.4

Special levies may also be raised from time to time where justified as a result of maintenance work or necessary repairs, replacements or improvements to the building, in terms of the Management Rules.

9.5

The collection of the levies, and payment of all expenses, is administered by the building's Managing Agent on the instructions of the Trustees. Owners are at present given 7 days of grace at the beginning of each month, by which time the Managing Agent must receive all monies owed by the owner in respect of that month, plus any penalties that may have been levied. Any queries regarding levies and payments must be addressed to the Managing Agent.

9.6

Failure to pay monies promptly when due, shall result in the debiting of interest to the accounts concerned. The rate of such interest shall be determined from time to time in accordance with Management Rule 31 (6).

9.7

If due levies are not paid by the 7th day of a calendar month, a warning notice shall be sent out, for which a charge shall be levied by the managing agent, payable by the defaulter. Should non-or late-payment be repeated, debt collection action may be taken against defaulters, and the defaulting owner shall be liable for all costs as provided for in Management Rule 31.

9.8

Additionally, in the case of chronic late or non-payment, the owner's name shall be sent to a Credit Bureau for blacklisting at the discretion of the Trustees.

10. Domestics, Private Staff and Private Staff Quarters

10.1

An owner or occupier of a unit shall be responsible for the activities and conduct of his private employees, specifically and especially chars, maids and gardeners and such, and shall ensure that such employees understand and abide by any Rules, national or provincial legislation or local authority by-laws which shall affect the scheme.

10.2

Chars and gardeners are not allowed to loiter on the common property, nor cause undue noise within units or on the common property or elsewhere.

10.3

Any owner or occupier whose private employee consistently fails to abide by the Conduct Rules shall be requested to remove such employee from the property if so instructed in writing by the Trustees.

10.4

No owner or occupier of a unit shall request personal duties to be performed by any member of Highgrove staff employed by the Body Corporate during working hours. No resident or owner shall give instructions or request favours from any employee or security guard of Highgrove, either during or after working hours.

11. Firearms / Throwing of Stones / Fireworks

11.1

No firearms, pellet guns or fireworks shall be discharged in Highgrove.

11.2

No stones or solid objects shall be thrown or propelled in Highgrove.

12. Fire Hoses

12.1

An owner or occupier of a unit shall not under any circumstances, except in the case of a fire, break the seal on any of the fire hoses in the complex. Any person found tampering with the seals or breaking the seals or in any way using the fire hoses for any other purpose other than that for which it was intended, will be fined the fee it will cost the Body Corporate to have the fire hose resealed.

13. Gardening

13.1

An owner or occupier of a unit shall maintain his exclusive use area for garden purposes, if any, in a neat and tidy condition.

13.2

In the event that employees of Highgrove or any agents appointed to look after the gardens mow lawns, the lawns will not be mowed if animal excrement is evident on the lawn. Owners will be responsible for removing all animal excrement before lawns are mowed. Should excrement not be removed, the owner will be responsible for the mowing of the lawn.

13.3

No plant or flower shall be picked from, nor shall any damage be caused to, the garden areas on the common property, which are not part of any exclusive use area, and the natural fauna and flora shall not be destroyed, removed or damaged in any way without prior written consent of the Trustees.

13.4

Garden tools and other equipment shall not be kept in any place where they will be viewed from other units or any portion of the common property.

13.5

Watering of gardens is to be done by the owners, and all local authority Rules or special regulations regarding water restrictions, should they exist, are to be adhered to. The Trustees or the Managing Agent will not be liable for any fines imposed by the local authority on any owner or occupant found not adhering to special regulations such as water restrictions.

13.6

Wall plants and creepers shall be properly maintained and not be allowed to cause damage to walls, brickwork or other parts of the structure and shall be cut back at the request of the Trustees. Any damage caused will be repaired for the account of the resident responsible.

13.7

Residents watering plants on balconies are to ensure that no excessive over-watering occurs which will cause inconvenience or damage to units below.

14. Indemnity

14.1

The Body Corporate / Managing Agent / Supervisor / Trustees and / or Body Corporate staff shall not be liable for any injury or loss or damage of any description which any owner or occupier of a section or any member of his family, his employee or staff or his relative, friend, acquaintance, visitor, failure to prevent shall sustain physically or to their property, directly or indirectly, in or about the common property or in the individual units, or for any act done or any neglect on the part of the Body Corporate or any of the Body Corporate employees, staff, agents or contractors.

14.2

The Body Corporate or its agents and staff shall not be liable or responsible in any manner whatsoever for the receipt or the non-receipt and the delivery or non-delivery of goods, postal matter or other property.

15. Insurance Claims

15.1

All insurance claims related to the building, common property and improvements thereon are to be handled solely through the Board of Trustees and the Managing Agent, as appointed from time to time.

15.2

Any damage to a section that is subject to a possible insurance claim shall be reported to the Managing Agent within twenty four hours of the event having occurred.

15.3

It is the section owner's responsibility to take all reasonable precautions to prevent loss, damage, destruction to his section and the common property. (The Body Corporate insurance policy does NOT cover the personal belongings of residents and residents are strongly advised to keep the contents of their section adequately insured in the event of fire, theft or any other unforeseen eventuality.)

15.4

Any insurance excess payable in respect of an insurance claim submitted to the Body Corporate's Insurer as a result of a burst geyser or any claim arising from or related to the geyser supplying hot water to the unit, shall be settled in full by, and be for the account of the owner of the unit concerned.

15.5

Residents are personally responsible for insuring the movable contents of their sections and for paying the premiums of such insurance.

16. Laundry

16.1

An owner or occupier of a unit shall not, without the prior written consent of the Trustees, erect his own washing lines, nor place any washing or laundry or any other items on any part of the building or the common property where it is visible from outside the building or from any other section. Any laundry left outside a unit not in the designated areas will be removed without consent.

16.2

Any washing hung out to dry in the designated washing line areas is done so at the sole risk of the owner thereof.

17. Letting of Units

17.1

All tenants of units or other persons granted rights of occupancy by any owner of the unit are obliged to comply with these Conduct Rules, notwithstanding any provision to the contrary contained in any lease or any grant of rights of occupancy.

17.2

The owner of a unit shall be obliged to provide the tenant with a copy of the Conduct Rules, obtainable from the supervisor or Managing Agent, and ensure that the tenant of his unit or other person granted rights of occupancy by him is obliged to comply with these Conduct Rules, notwithstanding any provision to the contrary contained in any lease or any grant of rights of occupancy.

17.3

For security reasons, the owner of a unit, the agents managing the lease or the prospective tenant shall be obliged to notify the Trustees in writing within 14 days of the date of conclusion of a lease of his unit of the full names of the tenant and of the period of the lease. Similarly, an owner shall forthwith notify the Trustees in writing of the full names of any other person granted rights of occupancy.

17.4

The number of persons who permanently reside in a section shall be limited to and in terms of the applicable local authority or any by-law.

18. Littering

18.1

An owner or occupant of a unit shall not deposit, throw or permit or allow to be deposited or thrown, on the common property any rubbish, including excrement, dirt, cigarette butts, food scraps or any other litter whatsoever.

19. Motor Vehicles, Use of Driveways, Parking Areas and Garages

19.1

Road signs on the common property shall be observed, and in particular the speed limit of 20 km/h.

19.2

Vehicles shall not be driven within the common property in any manner, which creates a nuisance or is considered by the Body Corporate not to be in the interest of safety.

19.3

Unlicensed persons are not permitted to drive any vehicle within the common property.

19.4

Hooters shall not be sounded within the common property other than in emergencies.

19.5

Vehicles shall be parked only on such areas of the common property as are specifically indicated or approved by the Body Corporate for that purpose and in such a way that the flow of traffic and access to and egress from garages, carports and parking bays is not obstructed.

19.6

One vehicle shall not occupy two visitors parking bays.

19.7

Two vehicles shall not be parked in one parking bay or garage if they protrude beyond the designated parking area.

19.8

A resident shall not at any time use a carport or parking bay other than for the parking of a motor vehicle. No parking bay or carport shall be used for the dumping of builder's rubble or any other rubbish, or for storage purposes.

19.9

Carports are reserved strictly for the use of owners and / or residents permanently residing in the building. Visitors shall only park in the identified visitors parking areas.

19.10

Damaged vehicles, vehicles that are not in general use, vehicles that drip oil or brake fluid on to the common property (including carports) or that are not roadworthy shall not be parked on the common property other than for such short periods as shall be approved by the Trustees in writing.

19.11

No trucks, caravans, trailers, boats or other heavy vehicles shall be parked on any portion of the common property without the prior written consent of the Trustees. Any of the above mentioned shall be registered with the Trustees and display the Highgrove residents' disk indicating unit number and registration number. The Trustees shall instruct any of the above vehicles parked, standing or abandoned on the common property in contravention of these Rules to be removed or towed away, at the risk and expense of the owner.

19.12

No person shall dismantle or effect major repairs to any vehicles on any portion of the common property, including carports.

19.13

Washing of vehicles is permitted only in the designated wash bays as per Annexure "B", but fire hoses shall under no circumstances be used for this purpose.

19.14

Trustees shall instruct any vehicles parked, parked on yellow lines or no parking areas, standing or abandoned on the common property in contravention of these Rules to be removed or towed off the property, at the risk and expense of the owner.

19.15

Parking of vehicles upon the common property is subject to the express condition that every vehicle is parked at the risk and responsibility of the owner of such vehicle and that no liability shall attach to the Body Corporate or its agents or any of their employees for any loss or damage of whatever nature which the owner, or any person claiming having been parked on the property.

19.16

The owner of the vehicle shall attend to car alarms that are triggered in property. Should the owner of the vehicle be absent from the complex, and the vehicle alarm causes a disturbance to the residents in the complex, the supervisor will be entitled to have the alarm switched off in whatever manner deemed fit at the expense of the owner.

20. Move-Ins and Move-Outs

20.1

The supervisor of the Body Corporate is responsible for ensuring that owners, tenants, domestic staff and contractors do not damage the passage walls, entrance foyers or stairs or any other part of the common property when moving in and out of the building. The supervisor shall, together with the party concerned, inspect the route to be followed and to record that there are no blemishes, marks, scratches or other damage on that route. In the event of there being scratches, blemishes, marks or any other damage it will be recorded in writing and signed by the owner or the contractor or the domestic staff member as well as the supervisor. Should it become apparent after the move in or move out or completion of the work by the contractor or others that damage has been caused, the owner concerned shall be responsible to the Body Corporate for any repairs required.

21. Noise

21.1

An owner or occupier of a unit shall ensure that he and his visitors and guests do not make or cause undue noise at all times. This rule applies to the building, common property and the swimming pool areas. Excessive noise, which is deemed to be a nuisance in the opinion of the Trustees, is not permitted.

23. Pets

23.7

Cats, which have not been neutered or spayed, shall not be kept in the complex. Certificates guaranteeing spaying / neutering shall be submitted to the Trustees / Managing Agent when requesting permission to keep a cat. Should animal be too young to be spayed / neutered, a certificate proving the spaying / neutering should be handed to the Trustees / Managing Agent within a year of gaining permission to keep the animal. Should such a certificate not be provided, the cats will be removed at the cost of the owner of the cat.

23.8

Bitches on heat shall not allowed on the common property at any time.

23.9

Vicious animals of any nature shall not be kept in any unit or on the common property.

23.10

Aviaries shall not be permitted, save with the consent of the Trustees.

23.11

The Body Corporate, Trustees, Managing agent or its appointed employees shall not be liable for any injury to any pet kept at the complex.

24. Refuse Disposal

24.1

Refuse is to be left on the ground floor outside units before 08h00 for removal by the Body Corporate staff from Mondays to Fridays. Refuse shall not be left on stairways, but shall be taken down to the ground floor for collection.

24.2

On Saturdays, Sundays, Public Holidays and after 08h00 on weekday mornings, when staff are not working or have already collected refuse, no refuse is to be left outside units. On these days, residents are to take their own refuse to the rubbish area at the entrance to the complex.

24.3

No refuse shall be left out overnight or placed in the corridors, behind corridor doors or on the stairs at any time.

24.4

Refuse that is left for removal shall be securely wrapped.

25. Signs and Notices

25.1

No owner or occupier of a unit shall place or permit to be placed, any sign, notice, billboard or advertisement of any kind whatsoever on or at that unit, or on or about any part of the common property, without the consent of the Trustees.

26. Slaughtering of Animals for Cultural, Religious or Other Purposes

26.1

At least two weeks written notice of the intended religious or cultural event requiring such ritual slaughter shall be given to the Trustees, specifying:
26.1.1 The date and time of the proposed slaughter;
26.1.2 the type of animal to be slaughtered;
26.1.3 the name and qualifications of the person who will be carrying out the slaughter;
26.1.4 confirmation that the animal will be brought on to the premises immediately prior to the ritual slaughter and that the carcass, and all remains of the animal, will be removed immediately from the premises after the act of ritual slaughter.

26.2

A notice from the local authority shall accompany the above notice confirming that all by-laws with regard to the ritual slaughter have been/will be complied with.

26.3

A notice from the health department shall accompany the above notice confirming that health department specifications with regard to the ritual slaughter have been complied with/will be complied with.

26.4

A certificate from the Society for the Prevention of Cruelty to Animals (SPCA) shall accompany the above notice confirming that an official from the SPCA will be present at the proposed event to ensure that the animal to be slaughtered does not endure unnecessary pain and suffering during such slaughter.

26.5

Notice shall also be given to all adjacent units of the date and time of the proposed slaughter.

26.6

Failure to comply with the above requirements shall entitle the Trustees of the Body Corporate to prevent the act of ritual slaughter from taking place on the premises or debiting the unit owner concerned with any costs incurred.

27. Storage of Flammable Material and Other Dangerous Acts

27.1

An owner or occupier shall not store any flammable materials, or do or permit or allow to be done, any dangerous act in the building or on the common property, which will or shall increase the rate of the premium payable by the Body Corporate on any insurance policy.

28. Swimming Pools and Entertainment Areas / Parties

28.1

The swimming pools are for the use of owners and occupiers of units but it shall be used by their visitors or guests provided that they are accompanied by an owner or occupier and that they comply with the Conduct Rules. Owners and occupiers of units are responsible for the behaviour of their visitors or guests and shall ensure that their number at any one time is not such so as to prejudice the comfort, enjoyment or convenience of other owners and occupiers wishing to make use of the pools. The Trustees reserve the right to restrict the use of the facilities by guests of owners/residents.

28.2

Radios, compact disc players, tape recorders and the like, as well as other musical instruments, are not permitted to be played at the swimming pools or at any other place on the common property so as to cause a disturbance or nuisance to other residents.

28.3

No alcoholic beverages are permitted in the pool area save and except on special occasions and then only with the prior written consent of the Trustees.

28.4

No glassware or bottles are permitted in the pool area.

28.5

Children under the age of 12 years using the swimming pool facilities must be supervised by an adult at all times.

28.6

The swimming pools shall not be used before 07h00 or after 21h00, unless special consent has been obtained from the Trustees.

28.7

The swimming pool and entertainment areas are used at the sole risk of the residents and their guests. The Body Corporate, its Trustees, employees or agents shall not be liable for any injury, loss or damage suffered by any person whilst in or around the swimming pool or entertainment areas.

28.8

The swimming pool and entertainment areas are not available for private parties unless prior written consent is obtained from the Trustees, which consent shall be subject to such conditions as the Trustees shall impose.

28.9

Functions, parties or social gatherings in the entertainment areas shall not continue after 22h00.

28.10

Residents intending to hold a function, social gathering or party in the entertainment areas shall give at least 48 hours written notice to the Trustees.

28.11

Residents hosting a function, social gathering or party in the entertainment areas shall ensure that such area is left in a clean and tidy condition after the function, failing which the Trustees shall be entitled to have the area cleaned at the cost of the resident concerned.
